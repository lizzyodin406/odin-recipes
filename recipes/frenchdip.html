French Dip Sandwich

French Dip is a popular sandwich created in Los Angeles during the early 20th century. It is unknown how the sandwich was made but there are some sources that believe it was named after the creators heritage. Other sources say it was named after the french roll it is often placed on.

The French Dip is known for consisting hot thinly sliced roast beef on a french roll or a baguette. It is often served with swiss cheese, onions, and some kind of dipping container filled with beef broth.

Ingredients

  • 5 pounds top or bottom round beef roast
  • 2 ounces au jus mix (two packages)
  • 1.4 ounces Italian dressing mix (two packages)
  • 32 ounces beef broth (1 tetra pak)
  • 8 hoagie buns
  • 8 slices Provolone cheese

Directions

  1. Spray the crockpot with nonstick spray
  2. Place the beef roast into the crockpot.
  3. Sprinkle the au jus mix and Italian dressing mix onto the beef roast.
  4. Pour the beef broth over the roast
  5. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours or until beef is fork tender.
  6. After cooking, remove roast and strain broth into a bowl. Broth can be used for french dip sandwiches or for open-faced roast beef sandwiches.
  7. Slice roast beef in thin slices
  8. Place roast beef and provolone on hoagie buns. Wrap sandwiches and warm in oven for 10 minutes at 400Â°F.
  9. Unwrap and enjoy! Use broth as dipping sauce.
